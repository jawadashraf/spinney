# Requirements Extract (From Project Brief Images)

Date: 2026-02-05

## 1. Background
- Spinney Hill delivers support services including drug and alcohol recovery, spiritual counselling, education and outreach, family support, food bank referrals, and community advice.
- Current operations are largely paper-based and disconnected, creating challenges for coordination, data capture, safeguarding, reporting, and long‑term follow‑up.
- A single integrated CRM is required to unite teams, enquiries, partners, and supporters into a secure shared platform.

## 2. Project Aim
Develop a secure, role‑based CRM system that:
- Centralises all enquiries and service user records.
- Enables teams to collaborate around each individual.
- Improves referrals, appointments, and follow‑up.
- Captures meaningful data for learning, reporting, and funding.
- Strengthens long‑term recovery and community impact.

The CRM should become the digital backbone of Spinney Hill.

## 3. Key Users
- Telephone / Frontline Enquiry Team
- Assessment Team
- Drug & Alcohol Workers
- Spiritual Counselling Team
- Education & Outreach Team
- Telephone Aftercare / Check‑In Team
- Management & Safeguarding Leads
- Admin & Fundraising Team

## 4. Enquiry Management (Front Door)
The CRM must log all incoming enquiries (especially telephone calls) under clear categories, including:
- Family members seeking advice or guidance
- Individuals seeking help for themselves
- School / Madrassa enquiries for seminars or workshops
- Donation offers (money, food, goods)
- Food bank support
- Domestic issues
- Mental health concerns
- General community support

For each enquiry, capture:
- Caller details
- Reason for contact
- Risk or safeguarding flags
- Advice given or action taken
- Referral made (internal or external)
- Staff member handling the enquiry
- Date and time

All enquiries must be stored for data analysis, reporting, and research.

## 5. Service User Registration & Case Management
When an enquiry becomes a client, the CRM should:
- Digitally register the service user
- Create a single client record
- Capture consent and GDPR status
- Record presenting issues and risks

Each service user record acts as a shared case file for relevant teams.

## 6. Appointments & Diary Management
The CRM must include shared diaries that allow:
- Telephone team to book appointments directly
- Appointments for:
  - Assessments
  - Drug & alcohol workers
  - Spiritual counselling
  - Education and support sessions

Bookings should trigger:
- Internal notifications
- Email confirmations (Phase 1)

## 7. Multi‑Team Notes & Collaboration
All teams working with a service user should be able to:
- Add case notes
- View relevant notes from other teams

Notes should be:
- Time‑stamped
- Linked to the author
- Categorised by service type

Sensitive notes (e.g., safeguarding, spiritual counselling) must have restricted access.

## 8. Telephone Check‑In & Aftercare (Follow-Up)
The CRM should support ongoing recovery and aftercare by:
- Giving telephone support staff access to contact details and engagement history.
- **Automated Follow-Up Tasks**:
    - **Active Service Users**: Adjustable follow-up schedule (every 1 week or 2 weeks).
    - **Post-Care (Inactive)**: Monthly follow-ups for service users who are "cured/sober" for less than 2 years.
- **Task Management**:
    - Generated follow-up tasks assigned to Check-In workers.
    - System alerts for due and overdue follow-ups.
- Allowing staff to log:
    - Welfare check‑in calls and follow‑ups.
    - Outcomes and next steps.

## 9. Directories & Relationship Management

### 9.1 Partner Organisations Directory
Central directory including:
- Name
- Address
- Contact details
- Type of support offered
- Referral criteria
- Notes and referral history

Enables faster, more consistent referrals and stronger partnerships.

### 9.2 Education & Outreach Directory
Directory of:
- Madrassas / Islamic schools
- Mainstream schools and colleges
- Youth organisations

Including:
- Key contacts
- Email addresses
- History of engagement

Supports newsletters, outreach campaigns, and education planning.

### 9.3 Donors & Supporters (Gift Aid)
Donor management capturing:
- Donor details
- Donation history
- Gift Aid consent and status
- Purpose of donations

Supports compliance, reporting, and fundraising strategy.

## 10. Access Levels & Permissions
Role‑based access examples:
- Telephone team: enquiries, bookings, check‑ins
- Practitioners: assigned service users and notes
- Safeguarding leads: full access and risk alerts
- Fundraising/Admin: donors and partners only

Ensures confidentiality, compliance, and safety.

## 11. Safeguarding, Consent & Compliance
System must include:
- Safeguarding flags and alerts
- Secure restricted notes
- Consent tracking for:
  - Data storage
  - Referrals
  - Communications
- Full audit trail of access and changes

## 12. Reporting, Data & Research
CRM must support reporting on:
- Volume and type of enquiries
- Service uptake and referrals
- Engagement and outcomes
- Donor contributions and Gift Aid

Dashboards for leadership and exportable reports for funders and trustees are highly desirable.

## 13. Enhanced Care & Operations (New)
### 13.1 Team & Task Management
- Teams must have tasks and individuals from those teams will be assigned to tasks.
- Task features: attention, alerts, and due time approaching notifications.
- Integrated Team management module.

### 13.2 Specialized Care Roles
- **Check-In Worker**: Regular logs of sessions and timeline access.
- **Spiritual Counselor**: Regular logs of sessions and timeline access.
- **Therapist**: 
  - Access to all notes.
  - Suggest actions for the service user.
  - Sessions can be charged (online/offline payments).

### 13.3 Dynamic Scheduled Forms
- Dynamic forms (surveys) generated for each service user.
- Daily and/or weekly actions for service users to perform and update.
- System reminders and motivation for service users.
- Automated alerts for relevant teams based on form activity.

### 13.4 Multi-Channel Notifications
- **Live Notifications**: Real-time alerts to relevant roles with actionable links.
- **Targeted Database Notifications**: Historical alerts that can be filtered.
- **Email Notifications**: For relevant roles, persons, or teams.

### 13.5 Payment Tracking
- Charging logic for Therapist sessions.
- Support for multiple payment methods: Online, Offline, Bank Transfer.

### 13.7 AI Engagement (Chatbot)
- Automated chatbot to engage website visitors enquiring about services.
- Intelligent information capture (name, contact details, reason for enquiry).
- Seamless integration with the Enquiry management module (creating automated enquiry records).

## 14. Budget & Engagement
- **Total Project Fee**: £4,000.
- **Project Duration**: 6-8 weeks.
- **Payment Structure**: Installment-based (linked to milestones).

## 15. Success Criteria
- All enquiries (manual and chatbot-generated) are logged in one place.
- Teams collaborate on shared client records with clear session histories.
- Service users engage with dynamic daily/weekly surveys.
- Payments and charges are tracked accurately.
- Staff experience reduced admin and better coordination through real-time notifications, role-specific dashboards, and automated chatbot leads.
